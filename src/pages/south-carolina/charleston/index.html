What To Do Immediately After Water Damage

In Charleston's climate, mold can begin forming within 24–48 hours. Acting in the first 60 minutes can significantly affect both the physical damage outcome and your insurance claim.

Short Answer: If water enters your home, stay out of any areas where electricity and standing water may mix, document damage with photos and video, stop the water source if you can safely do so, and contact a licensed water damage restoration company as quickly as possible.

  • 1
    Shut off electricity to flooded areas Only if safe to do so without entering standing water. Electrical hazards in water can be life-threatening.
  • 2
    Avoid contact with contaminated water Category 2 or 3 water (grey water, sewage, floodwater) carries serious health risks. Use PPE if contact is unavoidable.
  • 3
    Stop the water source if possible Shut off the main valve for burst pipes. Limits total water volume and supports your insurance claim.
  • 4
    Document all damage immediately Time-stamped photos and video before moving anything. Critical for insurance claim support.
  • 5
    Move valuables to dry areas Documents, electronics, and irreplaceable items should be relocated to reduce secondary losses.
  • 6
    Open windows if outdoor humidity is lower Passive airflow helps — but is not a substitute for professional drying in Charleston's humid climate.
  • 7
    Contact a licensed restoration professional Professional structural drying and moisture mapping significantly reduces mold risk in Charleston's 50–65% humidity environment.
  • 8
    Notify your insurance carrier promptly Most policies require timely notice. Delays may complicate or reduce your claim.

Do not use standard household fans as a substitute for professional dehumidification. In Charleston's humid climate, recirculating interior air without moisture extraction can slow effective drying and extend mold risk windows. Licensed restoration professionals use industrial-grade air movers and calibrated moisture meters per IICRC S500 protocols.

Signs Water Damage May Be More Serious Than It Looks

Water does not announce itself. Some of the most costly damage in Charleston homes develops behind surfaces that appear dry. If any of the following are present, professional moisture detection is recommended.

👃

Musty or Earthy Odor

One of the most consistent indicators of active mold growth behind walls, under flooring, or in crawl spaces. Develops quickly after even minor water events in Charleston's climate.

🎨

Bubbling or Peeling Paint

Indicates moisture migrating through drywall or plaster. Common in coastal Charleston homes after tidal flooding or storm events.

🪵

Warped or Buckled Flooring

Wood and laminate flooring absorbs moisture and swells. Cupping or buckling suggests sub-floor saturation — common in West Ashley homes with crawl space foundations.

💧

Ceiling Stains or Soft Spots

Yellowish-brown discoloration or soft areas may indicate ongoing or resolved roof leaks with retained moisture — especially relevant after Charleston's storm season.

🧱

Efflorescence on Foundation Walls

White mineral deposits on crawl space or basement walls suggest water regularly migrating through the foundation — a frequent issue in Charleston's water-saturated coastal soils.

📊

Unexplained Rise in Indoor Humidity

A persistent increase in indoor relative humidity without clear cause may reflect hidden moisture releasing from building materials — particularly in older Charleston bungalows and single-family homes.

Frequently Asked Questions

Water damage questions specific to Charleston and West Ashley homeowners

How long does water damage take to dry in Charleston?
Professional structural drying in Charleston typically takes 3–5 days under active remediation using industrial air movers and dehumidifiers. Charleston's high ambient humidity — averaging 50–65% — slows natural evaporation compared to drier climates. Larger jobs or Category 2–3 water contamination may take longer. Drying logs document daily moisture readings per IICRC S500 protocols.
How quickly can mold grow after water damage in Charleston?
Mold can begin forming on wet organic materials within 24–48 hours in Charleston's warm, humid conditions. Charleston's average humidity of 50–65% makes this timeline especially relevant. Waiting until visible mold appears before acting typically means remediation will be more extensive and costly than early intervention.
Does homeowners insurance cover tidal flooding in Charleston?
Standard homeowners policies generally exclude tidal flooding, storm surge, and riverine overflow. These require a separate flood insurance policy through the National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP) or a private insurer. Given that over 75% of Charleston properties carry meaningful flood risk, this gap is significant for many homeowners. Verify your FEMA flood zone at the FEMA Map Service Center.
Why are West Ashley crawl space homes at higher moisture risk?
West Ashley's older housing stock sits predominantly on crawl space foundations over coastal soils with a high water table. Without properly installed vapor barriers, functional sump pumps, and adequate ventilation, these spaces accumulate moisture even without visible flooding. Aging cast-iron drain lines and original plumbing without modern shut-off redundancies add to the risk profile. A slow leak in a West Ashley bungalow may go undetected for weeks — long enough for mold colonization to begin within structural framing.
Can water under floors cause mold?
Yes. Sub-floor saturation creates an enclosed, high-humidity environment where mold may develop within 24–48 hours. In Charleston homes with wood subfloors and floor joists, prolonged moisture can also cause wood rot and structural weakening. Thermal imaging and moisture meters are typically required to accurately assess sub-floor saturation.
What should I do first if my Charleston home floods?
Prioritize safety first — do not enter areas where electrical hazards may exist. If the source is internal, shut off the main water supply if accessible. Document all visible damage with time-stamped photos and video before moving items. Contact your insurance carrier promptly, and arrange for professional water extraction and structural drying as quickly as possible. Early mitigation is typically viewed favorably in the claims process.
Why is Charleston ranked among the highest mold-risk cities in the U.S.?
Charleston's combination of coastal geography, 48 inches of annual rainfall (26% above the U.S. average), 50–65% average relative humidity, high water table, and a ~5-year tropical cyclone recurrence interval creates persistent moisture risk. The city ranks approximately 17th among U.S. cities for mold risk. These are structural climate conditions — not isolated events — meaning ongoing exposure even outside major storm events.
